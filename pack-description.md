# Project Titan

A NeoForge 1.21.1 tech-progression modpack built around the **Titan Core** — a 10-tier crafting station whose appetite for resources and energy grows by an order of magnitude each tier, dragging the world's difficulty up alongside it and ending in a fight that's meant to hurt.

## What you're actually doing

You're feeding a machine.

The Titan Core crafts ten shards. Each shard is the gate to the next tier of the main chapter, and each one costs more than the last — meaningfully more, not "a few more diamonds" more. By the late game you are running real infrastructure to make a single craft happen.

To keep that pressure honest, the pack changes two things you might be used to leaning on:

- **Vanilla ore-gen is off.** Ores spawn as large deposits (AdLods), surfaced by flower indicators and located with handheld scanners. You go find them; they don't carpet your strip-mine.
- **Auto-mining is off.** Quarries, drills, "leave it running overnight and come back to a double chest of iron" — none of that. If you want more ore, you go get more ore, or you build a system that turns one resource into another.

## The world levels up with you

Every few tiers of the Titan Core, **Apothic World Tier** ticks up: mob HP, damage, and affix rarity all scale. The further you push the Core, the meaner the overworld gets. This is automatic — you don't opt in.

## The quests are a tour, not a checklist

There's an FTB Quests book covering energy, resource generation, gear, magic, combat, travel, and utility. Read it as a museum guide:

> "Here's what this mod does. Here's the machine it wants you to build. Here's the thing it wants you to make with it."

The quests never tell you *which* mod to use for a tier of the Core. That's your call — whatever fits the problem in front of you.

## Titan Trial — the endgame

Clearing Tier 10 hands you a key to the **Titan Trial**: a single-arena boss fight against three of the nastiest things in the modpack at once, scaled up further on the pack side. Survive it, and you walk away with the **Crown of the Titan**. That's the run.

## Why another kitchen sink?

Most progression packs I've played have a soft ceiling. A modest amount of overworld scavenging unlocks every craft you'll ever need, and one mid-tier generator powers the whole rest of the playthrough. End-game machines and end-game gear exist, but nothing in the pack actually requires them, so most players never build them.

Project Titan exists because I wanted a pack where you have to. The late-tier crafts are designed to outpace a mid-tier setup — so the end-game mods stop being optional showpieces and start being required.

## State of the pack

**1.0 — stable and playable front to back.** Still actively tuning, though, so a few things may shift:

- Titan Core recipe costs per tier
- Quest rewards (especially the early/mid tiers)
- Quest descriptions — most are AI-drafted first passes and could use a human read-through

If you find a tier that feels wrong, or a quest description that reads like a robot wrote it (because one did), I want to hear about it.
